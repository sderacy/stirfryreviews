Mi casa, tu casa, Sottocasa

I recently had the pleasure of dining at Sottocasa, a cozy pizzeria located in the heart of Harlem, New York. As a self-proclaimed pizza lover, I was excited to try their wood-fired Neapolitan-style pies, and I must say that I was not disappointed.

To start off, I ordered the burrata appetizer which was served with grilled bread, cherry tomatoes, and basil. The burrata was creamy and rich, and paired perfectly with the fresh and flavorful tomatoes. The grilled bread added a nice crunch to the dish, and the basil brought a fragrant and herbaceous note to the plate.

For my main dish, I ordered the classic Margherita pizza, which was cooked to perfection in the wood-fired oven. The crust was thin and crispy with a perfectly charred edge, and the tomato sauce was bright and tangy. The mozzarella cheese was fresh and melted beautifully, and the basil added a refreshing and aromatic touch to the pie.

I also tried the Diavola pizza, which had a spicy kick from the pepperoni and chili flakes, and was equally delicious. The toppings were fresh and of high quality, and the pizzas were served piping hot, straight out of the oven.

To accompany my meal, I ordered a glass of red wine, which paired nicely with the pizzas and added a nice balance to the flavors.

The service at Sottocasa was attentive and friendly, and the atmosphere was cozy and inviting, with warm lighting and a rustic decor.

Overall, I would highly recommend Sottocasa to any pizza lover looking for a delicious and authentic Neapolitan-style pie in Harlem. The quality of the ingredients and the wood-fired cooking technique make for a truly exceptional dining experience.

- Stirfry
